Savitribai Phule

1831 - 1897

Mother of Indian Feminism

Savitribai Phule was an Indian social reformer, educator, and poet. She is regarded as one of the most influential figures in the women's rights movement in India. Savitribai Phule played a crucial role in advocating for women's education and fought against social discrimination prevalent in the 19th century. Savitribai Phule was born on January 3, 1831, in Naigaon, Maharashtra, India. She belonged to a low-caste family, and her early life was marked by poverty and social marginalization. Despite these challenges, she displayed a strong desire for education and an unwavering commitment to social reform. In 1848, Savitribai Phule, along with her husband Jyotirao Phule, established the first school for girls in Pune, Maharashtra. This institution was a significant milestone in the field of women's education in India, as it provided an opportunity for girls from marginalized communities to access education.
